# Vivadeo Design Notes

Vivadeo should feel light, warm, editorial, and tactile. The product is a premium video archive and workflow tool, not a generic SaaS dashboard.

## Brand

- Brand name is always `Vivadeo`.
- Reference designs may use other names, but those names must never be copied into product UI.
- Subject imagery stays placeholder-based unless real product media is explicitly needed.

## Palette

Use only the Vivadeo palette:

- Grain: `#D7CEC7`
- Blackboard: `#565656`
- Oxblood: `#76323F`
- Tan: `#C09F80`

Do not add extra accent colors outside grain, blackboard, oxblood, and tan.

## Visual Direction

- Prefer creamy backgrounds, soft shadows, thin borders, and restrained depth.
- Typography should feel elegant but readable, with high contrast and clear hierarchy.
- UI should feel editorial and tactile, with composed spacing and intentional hierarchy.
- Placeholder imagery can be abstract blocks, collage shapes, or neutral mock art.
- Placeholders should look intentional, not like empty boxes.
- Use varied placeholder compositions so pages do not repeat the same visual language.

## Anti-Slop Rules

- No generic dark SaaS gradients.
- No glassmorphism by default.
- No neon accents.
- No purple bias.
- No default system-font-looking surfaces when typography is part of the experience.
- No oversimplified dashboard templates that erase product character.
- No repeated identical card grids unless the data demands it.

## Layout Language

- Landing should feel like a premium product brochure and archive catalog.
- Dashboard should feel like a control room with distinct zones, not a flat admin page.
- Search should feel like a curated results wall with side filters and a ranked feed.
- Auth pages should feel cinematic and calm, with one strong visual split.
- Cards should serve real repeated items, panels, or controls; avoid card-on-card layouts.
- Keep information dense enough for repeated operational use, while preserving the editorial warmth of the brand.

## Interaction And UI Detail

- Preserve clear workspace context on operational pages.
- Keep job, ingest, search, and clip workflows direct and scannable.
- Use restrained depth: soft shadows and thin borders should support hierarchy, not decoration.
- Avoid large decorative elements that compete with video/search content.
- Buttons, filters, status chips, and rails should remain readable against creamy backgrounds.
